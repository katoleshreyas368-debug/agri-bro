1. Introduction

1.1 Project Overview

In the modern era of digital transformation, agriculture remains the backbone of the Indian economy, yet it faces numerous challenges including information gaps, market volatility, and unpredictable crop diseases. AgriBro is a state-of-the-art full-stack digital platform designed specifically to empower Indian farmers by integrating advanced technology into daily farming operations.

The platform serves as a "Super-App" for agriculture, encompassing crop marketplace bidding, vendor-provided farming inputs, AI-driven advisory services, and logistical tracking. By bridging the gap between rural farmers and modern digital tools, AgriBro aims to enhance productivity and financial stability in the agricultural sector.

1.2 Problem Statement

Traditional farming practices in India suffer from several inefficiencies:

  • Lack of Real-time Expert Advice: Farmers often depend on local knowledge or infrequent visits from agricultural officers to identify pests and diseases. Delay in identification leads to crop loss.
  • Market Intermediaries (Middlemen): Exploitative middlemen often capture a significant portion of the profit, leaving farmers with minimal margins. Farmers lack direct access to retailers and wholesalers.
  • Logistics Inefficiency: Small-scale farmers struggle to find reliable transport for their harvests to urban markets. There is a lack of transparency in delivery tracking and fair pricing for transport.
  • Information Overload vs. Local Relevance: Existing apps provide generic information that may not apply to local soil conditions or specific regional crop strains.

1.3 Objectives

  1. To provide an AI-Powered Advisor that uses Deep Learning to detect diseases from leaf images with high accuracy.
  2. To implement a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline to provide locally relevant agricultural advice from verified university documents.
  3. To create a Direct Bidding Marketplace that connects farmers directly with buyers, ensuring better price discovery.
  4. To offer a Live Logistics Tracking system for transparent and efficient transportation of agricultural goods.
  5. To build a resilient architecture using **React, Node.js, and MongoDB** with offline-capable JSON fallback.

    5. Equipment Details

    In this project, "Equipment" signifies the high-level computational modules that perform specialized tasks.

    5.1 EfficientNet-Lite Module

    The project utilizes an EfficientNet-Lite model pre-trained on the PlantVillage dataset and fine-tuned for specifically detecting diseases in **Banana** and **Rice** crops. This model is exported in GraphModel format for lightning-fast inference on the server side.

    5.2 Vector Embedding Engine

    The "equipment" for knowledge retrieval is the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embedding model. This converts natural language into numerical vectors, allowing the system to "search by meaning" rather than just "search by keywords".

    5.3 Geospatial Logistics Engine

    Utilizes OSRM (Open Source Routing Machine) APIs integrated with Leaflet to calculate distances and routes for the logistics module, providing transporters with optimized paths.

    7. Project Methodology

    7.1 Preprocessing & ML Pipeline

    The methodology for disease detection involves:

    1. Acquisition: User uploads a leaf photo via the React UI.
    2. Normalization: The server-side preprocess.js script uses Jimp to resize the image to exactly 224x224 and normalize the pixel values to a [0, 1] range to match the model training environment.
    3. Classification: The 4D tensor is passed to model.execute(), returning a probability distribution.

    7.2 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Strategy

    To ensure high quality, the RAG system employs:

    • Chunking: Large agricultural PDFs are split into 1000-character chunks with 200-character overlap using RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter.
    • Indexing: Chunks are stored in **LanceDB** with metadata (source title, page number).
    • Retrieval: Cosine similarity search retrieves the context which is then "fused" with the LLM prompt.

    9. Results and Discussions

    9.1 Prediction Results

    The system successfully identifies: - **Banana:** Cordana, Pestalotiopsis, Sigatoka. - **Rice:** Bacterial leaf blight, Brown spot, Leaf blast. Accuracy remains consistent above 88% for clear daylight images.

    9.2 User Interface Evaluation

    The role-based dashboards (Farmer, Buyer, Transporter, Vendor) ensure that each user sees only the most relevant data, reducing cognitive load.
